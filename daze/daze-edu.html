Educational Outreach

Mentorship

Most recently, Daze has been interested in inspiring young students to create and collaborate just as he first did in the 70s. He didn't think he would make a good mentor at first and surprised himself:

"My work with kids and students is not something I planned on happening. At first it was an opportunity, then I realized that it was something I was good at...I'm not using kids as my assistants; I'm trying to bring out their own creativity in what they do.""

He describes his teaching style as "do-it-yourself" which is an attitude he adopted early on in his career with his work on the streets.

"I encourage students to look at me as a door that they can walk through and come out with experience."

Daze goes to underdeveloped parts of the world and underserved neighborhoods to teach workshops and encourage learning through drawing and painting. He was also sent to Quito, Ecuador as part of the smARTpower initiative, a program with the Department of State's Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs, administered by The Bronx Museum of the Arts that sends artists abroad to work with local artists and young people around the world to create community-based art projects.

Local & International Programs

- In 2001 the city council of Kruezlingen, Switzerland invited Daze to do a youth workshop with the kids to create an urban art project.
- In 2004 he was invited to the Super Mercado de Hip Hop to host various lectures and workshops in Rio de Janeiro. The series of events was sponsored by the American Consulado Geral and CUFA, for the Hutuz Festival.
- He worked with students of Lawrence High School in 2014 to transform the wall outside the Addison's Museum Learning Center with a mural depicting scenes of student life.
- In 2016 he also worked with students of the Thrive Collective to paint the mural of Cycloptic Mermaid, one of the community walls at Coney Art Walls.
- Daze was invited to be part of a project was organized by The Museum of Public Art in Baton Rouge to use murals and public art as a tool for encouraging visitors to the central business district.
